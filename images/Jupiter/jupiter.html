Jupiter - The Giant Planet

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System and is known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for centuries. Jupiter is a gas giant and has a very strong magnetic field.

  • Size: 139,820 km in diameter
  • Water Content: Jupiter has no solid surface but likely has liquid hydrogen and helium in its atmosphere.
  • Distance from the Sun: 778.5 million kilometers (5.2 AU)
  • Orbital Period: 11.86 Earth years
  • Moons: 79 moons (including Ganymede, the largest moon in the Solar System)
  • Atmosphere: Primarily hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane, ammonia, and other gases.
