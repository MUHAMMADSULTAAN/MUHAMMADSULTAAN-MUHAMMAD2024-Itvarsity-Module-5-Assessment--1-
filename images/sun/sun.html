The Sun - The Heart of Our Solar System

The Sun

The Sun is a star at the center of our Solar System and provides the heat and light necessary for life on Earth. It is composed mainly of hydrogen and helium and produces energy through nuclear fusion. The Sun has an enormous impact on our climate and weather patterns.

  • Size: 1.39 million km in diameter (about 109 times that of Earth)
  • Water Content: The Sun does not have water, but it does contain some trace amounts of water vapor in its outer layers.
  • Distance from Earth: 149.6 million kilometers (1 AU)
  • Temperature: Approximately 5,500°C at the surface, and up to 15 million°C in the core
  • Age: Around 4.6 billion years
  • Energy Source: Nuclear fusion of hydrogen into helium in the Sun's core
  • Rotation: The Sun rotates on its axis roughly every 25 to 35 days, depending on the latitude.
