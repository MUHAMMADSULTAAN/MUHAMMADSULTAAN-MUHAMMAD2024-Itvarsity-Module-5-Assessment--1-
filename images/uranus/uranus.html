Uranus - The Tilted Ice Giant

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is classified as an ice giant. It is unique in that it rotates on its side, with its axis almost parallel to the plane of the Solar System. Its atmosphere is composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and water, ammonia, and methane ice.

  • Size: 50,724 km in diameter (about 4 times Earth's diameter)
  • Water Content: Uranus contains a significant amount of water, methane, and ammonia in its atmosphere and ice layers.
  • Distance from the Sun: 2.87 billion kilometers (19.2 AU)
  • Orbital Period: 84 Earth years
  • Moons: 27 known moons, including Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon.
  • Atmosphere: Primarily hydrogen and helium, with methane, giving the planet its blue color.
  • Unique Rotation: Uranus has an extreme tilt in its rotation, rotating on its side with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees.
