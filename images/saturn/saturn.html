Saturn - The Ringed Giant

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is best known for its spectacular system of rings. It's a gas giant with a composition primarily made of hydrogen and helium, and it has over 80 known moons. Saturn's rings are made up of ice and rock, and they span hundreds of thousands of kilometers in diameter.

  • Size: 116,460 km in diameter
  • Water Content: Saturn has no liquid water, but its moons (like Enceladus) may have subsurface oceans.
  • Distance from the Sun: 1.43 billion kilometers (9.58 AU)
  • Orbital Period: 29.5 Earth years
  • Moons: Over 80 moons, including Titan (the second-largest moon in the Solar System)
  • Atmosphere: Primarily hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane, ammonia, and other gases.
  • Rings: Saturn's iconic rings are made up of ice particles, dust, and small rock fragments.
