Venus - The Earth's Twin

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition. However, its surface is incredibly hot, with temperatures hot enough to melt lead, and its thick atmosphere is mainly composed of carbon dioxide, creating an extreme greenhouse effect.

  • Size: 12,104 km in diameter (similar to Earth’s size)
  • Water Content: Venus has no liquid water due to its extreme surface temperatures.
  • Distance from the Sun: 108 million kilometers (0.72 AU)
  • Orbital Period: 225 Earth days (one year on Venus)
  • Moons: Venus has no moons.
  • Atmosphere: Thick clouds of sulfuric acid and mainly carbon dioxide, contributing to a strong greenhouse effect.
  • Surface Temperature: Around 465°C (869°F), hotter than Mercury, despite being farther from the Sun.
  • Rotation: Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets (retrograde rotation), and its day is longer than its year.
