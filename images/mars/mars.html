Mars - The Red Planet

Mars

Mars is often called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish appearance, which comes from iron oxide (rust) in its soil. It has been a subject of scientific study, especially with the potential for human exploration.

  • Size: 6,779 km in diameter
  • Water Content: Evidence of frozen water at the poles; signs of past liquid water.
  • Distance from the Sun: 227.9 million kilometers (1.52 AU)
  • Orbital Period: 687 Earth days (about 1.9 Earth years)
  • Moons: 2 moons (Phobos and Deimos)
  • Atmosphere: Mostly carbon dioxide with traces of nitrogen and argon.
